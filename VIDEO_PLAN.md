# Video plan for the new courses

Lesson breakdown for the three new courses (`spam-calls-and-messages`, `ai-voices`,
`ai-images-and-videos`), each currently live with one empty unit and zero lessons. This is a
shot list — what to actually film — not a course/unit structure change (nothing here is created
in Firestore yet; lessons only get created once a real video exists for them, via the Admin
panel's "Assign to a lesson").

Each lesson below is sized to match the existing site's pattern: short, single-concept videos
(2-4 minutes), not one long video covering everything.

## Course: Spam Calls & Spam Messages

**Note on scope**: the existing "Spotting Scams" course already has a "Phone & Text Scams" unit
covering urgent language and caller verification — these lessons deliberately focus on the
*mechanics* of spam (robocalls, spoofing, smishing) rather than repeating that ground.

1. **What Robocalls Actually Are** — how automated dialers work, why you get so many, and why
   they're cheap for scammers to run at massive scale.
2. **Caller ID Can Lie** — how "spoofing" makes a call look like it's from a local number, a real
   business, or even a number you recognize.
3. **Spotting a Spam Text ("Smishing")** — the tells in a scam text message: shortened links,
   fake delivery notices, "your account is locked" messages.
4. **Blocking and Reporting** — using your phone's built-in tools to block a number and report
   spam, so it's a five-second habit instead of a chore.

## Course: AI Voices

*A full script for this course's opening lesson already exists (written earlier this session) —
covers the grandchild-emergency scam scenario end to end. Reproduced here as Lesson 1; the
others break the same material into shorter, individually postable pieces.*

1. **When "I Know That Voice" Isn't Enough Anymore** *(script ready)* — the full scenario: a
   cloned voice, an urgent request for money, and the one habit that defeats it (hang up, call
   back yourself).
2. **What AI Voice Cloning Actually Is** — plain-language explanation of how a few seconds of
   audio becomes a convincing fake, no technical jargon.
3. **The Red Flags, One at a Time** — urgency, unusual payment requests, and "don't tell anyone"
   — isolated and explained so they're easy to remember individually.
4. **Setting Up a Family Safe Word** — a concrete, practical walkthrough of picking one and
   agreeing on it with family, for anyone who wants a backup to "hang up and call back."

## Course: AI Images & Videos

1. **What a Deepfake Actually Is** — simple explanation of AI-generated fake photos and video,
   grounded in real examples (not sci-fi framing).
2. **Where You'll Actually Run Into Them** — fake celebrity product endorsements, fake news
   clips, doctored "before and after" photos in scam ads.
3. **Visual Tells (and Why They're Getting Harder to Spot)** — practical signs to look for today,
   with an honest note that this list will keep getting shorter as the tech improves.
4. **Verify the Source, Not the Image** — the one durable habit: checking whether a shocking
   video or photo is being reported by an actual, real news outlet before believing or sharing it.

## Suggested production order

Given "AI Voices" already has a ready-to-use script for its first lesson, that's the fastest
starting point. After that, either course can go in any order — there's no dependency between
them.
