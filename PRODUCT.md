# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Seniors are the primary audience — many encountering this kind of material for the first time, often via a senior living community's in-person course before finding the free online lessons. The job to be done is recognizing and defending against scam calls, phishing, and AI-generated deepfakes (voices, images, video) before they cause harm, not just watching a video once. A secondary audience of general-public learners uses the same free courses without the in-person on-ramp.

## Product Purpose

VeraBlock teaches practical internet-safety skills through free, structured video lessons — organized into courses, units, and lessons — paired with AI-generated practice quizzes and lightweight XP/streak progress tracking. It also runs a real in-person course for senior living communities. Success is measured as learning outcomes: courses and quizzes completed, streaks sustained. That engagement is treated as the proxy for the deeper goal — fewer people falling for scams and deepfakes in the real world.

## Positioning

Free video lessons built specifically for seniors, with a real in-person course option for senior living communities — not just another generic security-awareness site.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, warm, plainspoken. The tone is civic and institutional in the sense of being credible and unhurried — like a library or a public-health resource — but never clinical or corporate. It should read as a knowledgeable, patient neighbor explaining something, not a vendor selling protection.

## Anti-references

Not a corporate cybersecurity vendor: no fear-based dark UI, no generic enterprise "navy-and-lock-icon" SaaS look, no manufactured urgency. Also not childish edtech: no cartoon mascots or overly playful visuals that risk feeling patronizing to an adult, senior-heavy audience.

## Design Principles

Plainspoken trust over corporate polish — every screen should read like a knowledgeable, patient neighbor, not a security vendor pushing a product. Built for the audience in the room — larger text, higher contrast, and simple, unambiguous interactions are the default reader's needs, not an edge case to accommodate later. Learning that shows its progress — XP, streaks, and completion should feel like real evidence of growing capability, not gamification for its own sake. Warm without being cute — encouragement without infantilizing. One front door, two jobs — the same visual language should welcome a first-time visitor on the landing page and support a returning learner in the dashboard without feeling like two different products.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the floor, tuned for a senior-heavy audience: larger default text sizes, contrast beyond the bare minimum where feasible, generous touch/click targets, low-motion defaults that honor `prefers-reduced-motion`, and never relying on color alone to convey state.
